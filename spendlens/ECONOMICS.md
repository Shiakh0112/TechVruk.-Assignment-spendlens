# ECONOMICS.md

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to TechVruk

TechVruk's business is AI infrastructure consulting and credits management. A "converted lead" means a startup that books a consultation and then purchases TechVruk services.

**Reasoning:**
- Average startup AI spend: $1,500/mo (based on audit data — teams of 5–20 paying for 3–5 tools)
- TechVruk can typically find 20–35% additional savings beyond what SpendLens surfaces (enterprise negotiations, credits programs, usage optimization)
- Additional savings identified: ~$400/mo
- TechVruk charges a % of savings captured or a flat retainer: assume $200/mo retainer for 6 months = $1,200 LTV per client
- Alternatively, if TechVruk earns 15% of savings as a fee: 15% × $400/mo × 12 months = $720/year per client

**Conservative LTV estimate: $800 per converted client** (blended between retainer and savings-share models)

## Conversion Funnel Math

```
Visitors → Audit Completed → Email Captured → Consultation Booked → Credit Purchase

1,000 visitors
  × 25% complete audit = 250 audits
    × 30% capture email = 75 leads
      × 15% book consultation = 11 consultations
        × 40% purchase = 4–5 clients/month
```

**At $800 LTV per client:**
- 5 clients/month × $800 = $4,000/month revenue from SpendLens leads
- CAC of $15–30 per lead × 75 leads = $1,125–$2,250/month in acquisition cost
- **Gross margin on this channel: ~$1,750–$2,875/month**

This is profitable from month 1 at these conversion rates.

## What Would Have to Be True for $1M ARR in 18 Months

$1M ARR = ~$83,333/month in revenue.

At $800 LTV per client and 6-month average contract:
- Need ~104 active clients at any time
- Or ~208 new clients over 18 months (accounting for churn)

**The math:**

Month 1–3: Build to 500 audits/month → 150 leads/month → 22 consultations/month → 9 clients/month
Month 4–6: Scale to 2,000 audits/month via Product Hunt + newsletter → 600 leads/month → 90 consultations/month → 36 clients/month
Month 7–12: Compound via referrals + SEO → 5,000 audits/month → 1,500 leads/month → 225 consultations/month → 90 clients/month
Month 13–18: Steady state at 90 new clients/month, 40% churn = 54 net new/month → 104 active clients by month 18

**What has to be true:**
1. SpendLens drives 5,000+ audits/month by month 7 — requires viral sharing (OG images, X posts) and SEO traction
2. Consultation booking rate stays at 15% — requires the audit to be genuinely useful and the CTA to be well-placed
3. TechVruk closes 40% of consultations — requires a strong sales process and clear value proposition
4. Average contract value stays at $800+ — requires TechVruk to deliver real savings, not just advice

**The biggest risk:** Consultation booking rate. If it drops to 5%, the whole model breaks. The fix is making the high-savings CTA more prominent and the consultation offer more specific ("30-minute call, we'll find $X more in savings or it's free").

## Sensitivity Analysis

| Audit → Email Rate | Email → Consultation Rate | Clients/Month (at 5k audits/mo) | Monthly Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20% | 10% | 40 | $32,000 |
| 30% | 15% | 90 | $72,000 |
| 40% | 20% | 160 | $128,000 |

The 30%/15% scenario gets to $1M ARR in ~14 months. The 20%/10% scenario takes 24+ months.

**Conclusion:** $1M ARR in 18 months is achievable but requires hitting the middle scenario — which means investing in the audit quality and CTA design, not just traffic.
